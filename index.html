Malaysia’s Meat Dependency

Chicken dominates Malaysia’s meat basket, but beef and mutton reveal a deeper import-dependency problem.

Malaysia sits within a meat-consuming region

This map places Malaysia in a Southeast Asian context. Circle size shows total meat consumption per person, helping viewers compare Malaysia with nearby countries before focusing on Malaysia’s internal meat pattern.

Beef and mutton carry the highest supply risk

This heatmap compares each meat type across four simple risk indicators. Beef and mutton show high import dependency and supply risk, while poultry has stronger domestic supply strength.

Latest-year meat consumption ranking

This chart compares the latest-year meat consumption values by meat type. It shows whether poultry, beef, mutton or pork contributes most to Malaysia’s meat basket.

Meat consumption over time

This chart shows how Malaysia’s meat consumption changes by meat type over time. The lower timeline can be brushed to focus on a selected period.

Chicken takes the largest share of Malaysia’s meat basket

This stacked area chart shows the changing share of each meat type in Malaysia’s total meat consumption. It helps show whether the diet is becoming more poultry-heavy over time.

Poultry is close to self-sufficient, but beef and mutton are not

This chart compares each meat type against the 100% self-sufficiency reference line. Values below 100% suggest that local production does not fully meet domestic demand.

Self-sufficiency has stayed uneven across meat types

This chart tracks self-sufficiency ratio over time. Poultry remains near or above the 100% reference line, while beef and mutton stay far below it.

Beef and mutton show the clearest production-consumption gap

This chart compares domestic production with estimated consumption. A larger gap suggests stronger reliance on external supply or imports.

Beef and mutton imports outweigh exports

This diverging bar chart compares import and export values. Imports are shown to the right, while exports are shown to the left, making the trade imbalance easier to see.

Beef and mutton remain the most import-dependent meats

This heatmap shows import dependency ratio by meat type and year. Darker cells indicate stronger reliance on imports, making long-term supply vulnerability easier to compare.

The food security risk is concentrated in beef and mutton

This quadrant chart combines consumption, self-sufficiency and import dependency. Poultry is consumed the most and remains close to or above domestic self-sufficiency, while beef and mutton have much lower self-sufficiency and larger import dependence.

Malaysia’s meat basket is overwhelmingly poultry-heavy

This custom waffle chart translates the latest meat consumption pattern into 100 simple units. It makes the dominance of poultry easier to understand at a glance.
