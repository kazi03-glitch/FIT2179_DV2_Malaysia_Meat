Malaysia’s Meat Dependency

Poultry dominates Malaysia’s meat basket, while beef and mutton show a much stronger dependence on imports.

Malaysia sits within Southeast Asia

This map places Malaysia in its regional context before focusing on Malaysia’s meat supply pattern. Malaysia’s 2024 total meat consumption for the four selected meat types is shown using official livestock data.

Beef and mutton carry the highest supply risk

This heatmap compares each meat type across four simple risk indicators. The scores are derived from official 2024 consumption, self-sufficiency and import dependency data.

Latest official-year meat consumption ranking

Poultry has the highest per-person consumption in Malaysia. Pork is second, while beef and mutton remain much smaller parts of the meat basket.

Meat consumption over time

This chart shows official 2020–2024 consumption and simple 2025–2026 projections. Projected values are shown with a dashed line and should not be treated as official statistics.

Poultry takes the largest share of Malaysia’s meat basket

This stacked area chart shows the changing share of each meat type in total selected meat consumption. The basket remains strongly poultry-heavy.

Poultry is much closer to self-sufficient than beef and mutton

This chart compares each meat type against the 100% self-sufficiency reference line. Values below 100% show that local production does not fully meet domestic utilization.

Self-sufficiency remains uneven across meat types

Poultry remains far closer to the 100% reference line than beef and mutton. Projected values are included only to show possible short-term direction.

Beef and mutton show the clearest production-utilization gap

This chart compares domestic production with domestic utilization. A larger gap suggests stronger reliance on external supply or imports.

Beef and mutton imports outweigh exports

This diverging bar chart compares import and export volumes in tonnes. Imports are shown to the right, while exports are shown to the left.

Beef and mutton remain the most import-dependent meats

This heatmap shows the import dependency ratio by meat type and year. Darker cells indicate stronger reliance on imports. The final two years are projections.

The food security risk is concentrated in beef and mutton

This quadrant chart combines consumption, self-sufficiency and import dependency using 2024 official data. Beef and mutton sit lower on self-sufficiency and higher on import dependency.

Malaysia’s selected meat basket is overwhelmingly poultry-heavy

This custom waffle chart translates the 2024 official consumption pattern into 100 simple units. It makes the dominance of poultry easier to understand at a glance.

Note: 2020–2024 values are official published values. 2025–2026 values are simple linear projections based on the 2020–2024 trend and are not official statistics.

Data sources: Department of Veterinary Services Malaysia Livestock Statistics 2024, DOSM Supply and Utilization Accounts Selected Agricultural Commodities 2020–2024, and public geographic boundary data.
